Winter

THINGS TO CONSIDER
Before starting your winter garden, it’s a good idea to learn about the USDA Hardiness Zone in your area. That
information will help you understand the average low temperatures during the winter and which crops you can
grow without frost protection.


THINGS TO CONSIDER
Winter crops should be planted in late
summer to early fall for harvest during the
winter months.

WHEN TO HARVEST
Harvesting times depend on the type of crop your
growing and the required number of weeks it takes to
grow it successfully.

Spring

THINGS TO CONSIDER
Think about raising the beds in your garden. It will expand the growing area and also help your crops grow better.


THINGS TO CONSIDER
Plant cool-season vegetables before the last frost date and warm-season crops after that date. Sowing times are determined by the type of veggie you’re planting and the weather in your area.

WHEN TO HARVEST
The proper time to harvest your vegetables depends on the crops you sow, but most spring veggies mature between May and September.

Summer

THINGS TO CONSIDER
Diversify your crops so that you won’t have as many problems with pests. Also, find vegetation that's considered disease resistant.


THINGS TO CONSIDER
Summer veggies should be planted in the spring after the last frost. Most warm climate crops cannot survive cold weather and require temperatures between 65 to 90 degrees.

WHEN TO HARVEST
Summer crops produce late fall harvests; the amount of time needed for maturity depends on the type of vegetable that’s planted.

Autumn

THINGS TO CONSIDER
How you being you fall crops is determined by the climate in your region. If you live in hot weather, start your fall crops indoors until the temperature drops.


THINGS TO CONSIDER
Timing is very important for having a successful autumn garden, so begin by learning the first fall frost date in your area.

WHEN TO HARVEST
The amount of days needs to fulfill a harvest depend upon the crops you’ve planted. Some crops produce during the fall months and others in late winter/early spring.
